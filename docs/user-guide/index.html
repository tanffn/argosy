Argosy — User Guide (end-to-end)

How you actually use the whole system — not just one tab. Live screenshots, traced flows, and the holes I found while writing this.

Audience: Ariel (single-user mode) Live capture: 2026-05-28 via Playwright + Chromium Pages audited: all 14 nav tabs Bug fixes shipped: 6 retirement + 1 logged for /argonaut

1. What Argosy is

Argosy is two things glued together:

  1. An always-on Python orchestrator that watches your portfolio and the market continuously, with cadences running at minute / hour / daily / weekly / monthly / annual rhythms.
  2. A Next.js dashboard at localhost:1337 where you read state, approve proposals, and negotiate with the agent fleet.

The engine convenes a fleet of AI specialists (analysts, debate, risk officers, fund manager) to debate any non-trivial move. Each rationale is persisted, so months later you can ask why something happened and get a real answer.

The user-visible surface is split into 14 tabs, but the working flow only routes through 5-6 of them regularly. The rest are inspection / debugging tools.

3. How data gets in — the single mechanism

Tl;dr
Everything — bank statements, credit-card PDFs, screenshots, news, "I just got a bonus" — enters Argosy through /advisor chat. There is no separate upload form, no /expenses-import button, no Files-upload widget. The Advisor is the universal portal.

Two ways to give Argosy new information from this surface:

  1. Free-form text in the chat. "I just got a 50K USD bonus paid today" or "We're expecting our second kid in November" or "I'm switching from Migdal to Clal." The Advisor extracts structured fields and updates the relevant gaps + propagates to the projections.
  2. Attach a file. Click the Attach pill (text/markdown, images, or PDFs — 10 MB per file, 20 MB total). Drop a Leumi statement PDF, a Discount Mastercard statement, a Schwab CSV, a screenshot of your payslip. The ingestion pipeline parses it server-side; the catalog row appears in /files; the relevant dashboard surface (e.g. /expenses) refreshes once parsing completes.

What's IN the gap tracker

The right-side panel groups ~75 required fields under 11 stages (Identity · Goals · Financial picture · Broker connections · Plan · Ops prefs · Estate · Insurance · Tax · Education · Special situations). Each row is colored:

  • fresh — value is recent enough for the field's freshness policy (e.g. portfolio_value monthly, identity annually)
  • stale — past freshness window; should be refreshed
  • missing — never filled

Currently: 76 fresh · 0 stale · 1 missing. The 1 missing is the "Last-reviewed year of legal will" gap surfaced on the Home page.

4. End-to-end flow: First-time setup

STEP 1
Open /advisor
The intake panel is the first thing you touch. Gap tracker on the right shows all 75 fields, mostly red (missing).
STEP 2
Click any missing row in the gap tracker
e.g. "Tax residency" → chat focuses on that field, asks you to fill it. Answer in plain English. Argosy extracts the structured value + marks the row fresh.
STEP 3
Repeat — or paste a bulk update
You can answer rows one-by-one OR paste a multi-field message ("DOB 1982-08-28, Israeli citizen, married, two kids ages 12 and 9, primary residence in Kiryat Tivon").
STEP 4
Attach your portfolio TSV / Schwab CSV
Drag onto chat or click Attach. The ingestion pipeline catalogs it under /files and the values land on /portfolio.
STEP 5
Attach 6-12 months of bank + credit statements
Drop the PDFs one by one. The expense pipeline parses each and rows populate on /expenses.
STEP 6
Wait ~5-10 minutes for the first synthesis
The monthly_cycle loop kicks off a multi-agent synthesis once you have minimum-viable data. /plan populates with a draft.
STEP 7
Open /retirement
Now the long-term verdict has real data to chew on. Hero shows P(solvent at 95) under fat-tail-aware regime-switch MC.

5. End-to-end flow: "I just got a 50K USD bonus"

UX hole #1
There is no "+ Add income event" button anywhere in the UI. The user has to know that the Advisor chat is the universal entry point. A user might look for a button on /portfolio or /expenses and not find one.
STEP 1
Open /advisor
Don't click a gap-tracker row — just go to the chat textarea.
STEP 2
Type: "I just got a $50K bonus paid 2026-05-28."
Click Send. The agent classifies this as an income event, updates the rsu_grants block in identity_yaml if it's RSU, or creates a one-shot income event otherwise. Confirmation appears in the chat.
STEP 3 (optional)
Attach the payslip / wire confirmation PDF
If you have a document, drop it for audit-trail. Lands in /files with a link back to this turn.
STEP 4
Re-open /portfolio
The net worth + monthly income figures update once the wire credit hits the next portfolio snapshot. (If you want it reflected immediately, drop the latest Schwab balance.)
STEP 5
Trigger fires — check /
A large income event qualifies as a replan-trigger ("job_change" or "income_shock"). Home shows a banner; the verdict on /retirement re-runs automatically.
UX hole #2
Argosy doesn't tell the user explicitly that the projection re-ran. The Home banner shows the trigger fired but the user has no visible confirmation that the /retirement verdict has updated to reflect the bonus. Suggested fix: add a "Last re-computed at" stamp on the hero card.

6. End-to-end flow: Monthly bank statement update

STEP 1
Get your monthly PDFs
Leumi NIS checking, Leumi USD, Discount Mastercard, Cal, any credit card statements.
STEP 2
Open /advisor
Yes, again — there's no "import statement" button on /expenses or /files.
STEP 3
Click Attach (or drag) — one PDF at a time
10MB per file, 20MB per turn. Drop the May 2026 Leumi NIS statement. The parser (issuer-aware: Leumi/Discount/Cal/Schwab) recognizes the format and extracts transactions.
STEP 4
Wait for parse confirmation in chat
The Advisor responds with "Parsed 47 transactions from Leumi-NIS · May 2026" (or similar). If parsing fails, you'll see "?? UnknownFormatError" — drop a screenshot instead.
STEP 5
Repeat for every issuer
Discount, Cal, second card. Each turn = one statement.
STEP 6
Open /expenses
Monthly sub-tab now shows the new month populated. Categories auto-assigned via the household_categorizer agent.
UX hole #3 — no "last sync" indicator
/expenses shows the trailing-12 view but doesn't tell you when the latest statement was ingested. If you forgot to upload last month's Cal statement, the page silently shows incomplete data.
Suggested fix: per-source "Last statement: May 2026 (5 days ago)" indicator in the Sources sub-tab.
UX hole #4 — no upload widget on /files
/files is read-only. Users expect "Files = where I upload files." Instead it's "Files = catalog of what's already been uploaded." Misleading.
Suggested fix: add an Upload button on /files that uses the same catalog_upload backend the Advisor chat uses.

7. End-to-end flow: Credit card categorization

Argosy auto-categorizes each transaction via the household_categorizer agent (Opus-backed by default, per the accuracy-over-cost binding preference). Most rows are right out of the box; some need manual correction:

STEP 1
Open /expenses → Transactions sub-tab
Find the misclassified row (e.g. a hardware-store charge tagged "Other" should be "home_improvement").
STEP 2
Click the category cell — inline edit
Type the new category or pick from autocomplete. The choice is recorded in category_overrides and the agent learns from this for future similar merchants.
STEP 3 (alt)
Bulk-relabel via the Merchants sub-tab
If you want every "Cofix" charge to map to "coffee" instead of "restaurants", do it once at the merchant level and it back-propagates.

8. End-to-end flow: Plan → Retirement loop

You're right that the natural order is Plan first, then Retirement. They answer different questions:

PageAnswersTimescale
/plan"What should I DO this month/quarter?"Tactical · short + medium horizon
/retirement"Can I retire? When? At what risk?"Strategic · 30+ year horizon
STEP 1
Open /plan
Read the current draft. The FM agent's verdict (accept / reject) is at the top.
STEP 2
Walk the FM objections list
Each objection has AGREE / DISAGREE / DEFER + a counter-position textarea. The "Explain in plain English" toggle reveals a translated version if you don't follow the analyst jargon.
STEP 3
Hit "Start new round with my decisions"
Synthesis re-runs through all 5 phases (analysts → debate → synthesizer → risk → fund manager) with your AGREE/DISAGREE/DEFER decisions as user_directive. Takes ~70 min for the full Opus stack.
STEP 4
Once the new draft is accepted, open /retirement
The plan's accepted assumptions (mu, sigma, withdrawal policy, retirement age) are now your baseline. /retirement tells you whether those assumptions actually survive the 30-year fat-tail Monte Carlo.
STEP 5
If /retirement says OFF_TRACK, go back to /plan
Re-negotiate. Disagree with the FM objection that's now load-bearing (e.g. "delay retirement to 52"). The two pages bounce against each other until they agree.

9. / Home in detail

What each block does:

  • Anchor banner — version + agent count + cadence count + mode badges. Mode is paper / live / queue_only.
  • Fleet self-review — automatic post-synthesis audit of the agent fleet's own output (10 deterministic detectors). Link goes to /fleet-review/[id].
  • Daily-brief card — stitched from the day's gap deltas + investor-event signal + watchlist anomalies. "Review monthly plan" CTA → /plan.
  • Action items widget — pulled from the active plan's short + medium horizon. Bucketed OVERDUE / TODAY / DUE_SOON / UPCOMING.
  • Synthesis history (below the fold) — accordion of every prior agent-fleet run with cost + agent count + timestamp.

10. /advisor Advisor in detail

Layout:

  • Top banner — "Draft plan ready · 19 delta(s) vs. last month" + Review now CTA.
  • Plan in scope bar — which intake-upload doc is being used as the baseline + a Re-distill button.
  • Conversation panel — Cascade footer ("0 agents · $0.0000 · 0.0s") shows multi-agent cost of your last turn. Type a question, drop a file, paste a screenshot.
  • Attach pill — text/markdown, images, or PDFs. 10MB/file, 20MB/turn.
  • Gap tracker — 76 fresh / 0 stale / 1 missing. Rows grouped by stage. Click any row → chat focuses there.
Why this is the data portal
Every other dashboard READS from the data Argosy already has. Only the Advisor WRITES to it. Files = catalog (read-only), Expenses = parsed transactions (read-only), Portfolio = latest snapshot (read-only). If you want to change anything, you do it here.

11. /portfolio Portfolio in detail

This is the FIRE-position view. Different from /retirement:

This pageQuestion answeredEngine
/portfolioAm I at FIRE today, under simple 25-yr SWR math?Deterministic — 3 scenarios (bear 0%, conservative 2%, typical 4.5% real return) · SWR 3.5%
/retirementWill I stay solvent through 95 under fat-tail / FX / Israeli-tax / regime-switch risk?Stochastic — regime-switch MC + bootstrap CI + age-aware tax + stochastic FX

Both can coexist. /portfolio says "Already at target" under all three scenarios. /retirement says "OFF TRACK at 57% P(solvent at 95)". Both are right at their own level of conservativeness — the Portfolio chart shows the optimistic-deterministic story, Retirement shows the conservative-stochastic truth.

12. /expenses Expenses in detail

Sub-tabs:

  • Overview — what's shown above. Trailing 12 vs Calendar year toggle. Per-currency vs NIS-converted toggle.
  • Monthly — month-by-month bar chart + per-month drill. Where you spot anomalies.
  • Transactions — flat row-level view. Search + filter. Inline category-edit.
  • Sources — per-issuer health (Leumi-NIS · Leumi-USD · Discount · Cal · Schwab). Currently 6 sources.
  • Merchants — merchant-level rollup. Bulk relabel from here.
  • Trips — auto-detected travel periods (hotel + restaurant + transport cluster).
  • RSU — Schwab disbursements vs Leumi USD wire credits cross-validation.

What's missing: an Income sub-tab. RSU is there, but salary + dividends + bonus + side-income don't have a dedicated drill-down. Asymmetry with how detailed the expense side is.

13. /plan Plan in detail

The page lifecycle:

  1. Monthly cycle fires (1st of month) — 5-phase synthesis: 10 analysts → 2 debate teams → synthesizer → 3 risk officers → fund manager. ~30-70 min depending on stack.
  2. FM verdict lands — either ACCEPTED or REJECTED with N objections.
  3. Page renders the draft — rejection summary + per-objection details, severity-color-coded.
  4. Per-objection toggle — AGREE · DISAGREE · DEFER. DISAGREE opens a counter-position textarea. "Discuss with [analyst]" launches an FM↔analyst dialogue.
  5. "Start new round with my decisions" — re-runs synthesis with your toggle decisions as user_directive. Loop continues until FM accepts or you accept anyway.

Filters: Kind · Source · Include deleted toggle.

Each row shows: filename · Kind (pdf / image / ...) · Source bucket (chat_attachment most common) · Size · When · Open link.

UX hole #4 (repeated)
No upload widget here. Anything you want catalogued goes through the Advisor chat's Attach button. The Files tab is misleading by name — should probably be renamed "Catalog" or get a real upload button.

16. /proposals · /argonaut · /decide

The trade-decision flow:

  1. Submit a ticker via /decide — enter symbol + rationale → agent fleet generates Buy/Sell/Hold proposal.
  2. Proposal lands in /proposals.
  3. Approve / Reject / Escalate. For T0/T1 in Argonaut on live mode, the engine auto-executes.
  4. Filled orders surface in /argonaut (Recent trades) and /audit.
Bug found on /argonaut (logged separately)
Live audit captured a 404 on GET /api/plan/current/structured?user_id=ariel — endpoint doesn't exist. The Argonaut page tries to fetch the structured plan but the backend doesn't expose this shape. Doesn't break the page (graceful fallback to "no positions") but it's a real network-log bug.
Suggested fix: add the endpoint OR remove the fetch from the Argonaut page.

17. /agents · /audit · /domain-kb · /settings

Inspection / config tabs. Brief overview:

  • /agents — agent fleet activity log. Filter by role + time range. Use when debugging "why did the FM reject?"
  • /audit — flat audit_log table. Every decision, every event, queryable. Use for "what did Argosy do last Tuesday at 09:00?"
  • /domain-kb — Israel-resident knowledge base (tax brackets, UCITS rules, BL rates). Read-only reference.
  • /settings — per-feature config. Mostly leave alone after first setup.

18. Fixes shipped this round

All 6 retirement bugs/UX issues closed
Live A/B verifies the sigma pipe now works: σ=0.18 (default) → 92% UNCERTAIN; σ=0.344 (auto-calibrated NVDA-aware) → 48% OFF_TRACK. A 44pp swing the page was hiding.

Bug #1 — Sigma calibration result fed into the verdict FIXED

Route accepts sigma_annual query param. api.retirement.ruinProbability opts include sigmaAnnual. RuinProbabilityHero accepts sigmaAnnual prop. page.tsx fetches sigma calibration once on mount and threads the value to the hero. Net effect: changes to your portfolio composition now actually change the verdict number.

Bug #2 — Lump-vs-annuity redundant "split" text FIXED

Was rendering the recommendation enum ("split") as the card description. Now renders the explanatory sentence: "Annuity alone doesn't cover essential expenses but lump alone is risky. 50/50 hybrid hedges both ways: annuity provides a guaranteed floor; lump provides upside + flexibility."

UX #3 — "Coming up" card deleted FIXED

All 7 waves shipped. Stale card removed from page.tsx.

UX #4 — VPW/Bucket policy caveat FIXED

When VPW or Bucket is selected, an amber caveat appears below the radio list explaining that those policies eliminate literal zero-balance ruin but don't guarantee covering essential expenses. Users no longer think the selector is broken when the verdict stays OFF_TRACK.

UX #5 — Hero loading indicator FIXED

Separate loading state; prior data stays visible while re-fetching; small re-running… pulse badge appears next to the verdict label during the 1-2s MC re-run.

UX #6 — Page TOC sidebar FIXED

17 cards reorganized into 11 named sections with anchor IDs. Sticky left-side TOC visible on large viewports. Hidden on mobile to preserve vertical space.

19. Holes still in the flow (not yet fixed)

These are real gaps that the per-tab walkthroughs surfaced. None block usage; all worsen UX:

Hole #1 — "Add income event" isn't discoverable
Bonus / job change / income shock all go through Advisor chat. No button labeled "Add income event" exists anywhere.
Suggested fix: a "+ Add event" affordance on Home and/or Portfolio that deeplinks to /advisor?focus=income_event.
Hole #2 — No "verdict re-computed" confirmation
When a trigger fires, the verdict re-runs server-side but no visible "Last re-computed at 14:32 (3 min ago, triggered by: income_event)" stamp.
Suggested fix: stamp the hero with last-compute timestamp + trigger cause.
Hole #3 — /expenses has no "last sync" per source
If you forgot to upload May 2026's Cal statement, /expenses silently shows incomplete data.
Suggested fix: per-source freshness badge in the Sources sub-tab.
Hole #4 — /files is read-only despite the name
Files = catalog, not upload portal. Users naturally try to upload here.
Suggested fix: add an Upload button on /files that calls the same catalog_upload backend the Advisor chat uses, OR rename the tab to "Catalog."
Hole #5 — Parsing failures are silent
If a PDF is in an unsupported format (older SpreadsheetML Leumi files), the parser logs "?? UnknownFormatError" but the Advisor chat shows a generic message. No visible error in /files.
Suggested fix: per-file parse-status column on /files with a "Re-try" button.
Hole #6 — /argonaut 404 on /api/plan/current/structured
Already logged. Pre-existing endpoint gap, separate from retirement work.
Hole #7 — /expenses lacks an Income sub-tab
RSU has its own sub-tab but salary / dividends / bonus / side-income roll up into "Income" on Overview only. No drill-down. Asymmetry with how detailed the expense side is.
Suggested fix: add an Income sub-tab parallel to RSU.
Hole #8 — No site-wide search
If you're hunting for a specific past decision ("the day we trimmed NVDA in March"), you have to know to go to /audit. No site-wide search box.
Suggested fix: ⌘K / Ctrl-K palette searching audit_log + transactions + plans.
