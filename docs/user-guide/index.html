Argosy — User Guide (end-to-end)

How you actually use the whole system — not just one tab. Live screenshots, traced flows, and the holes I found while writing this.

Audience: Ariel (single-user mode) Live capture: 2026-05-28 via Playwright + Chromium Pages audited: all 14 nav tabs Recent shipments documented here: Windfall auto-detect flow (banner + card) · /expenses bank-statement upload tile · /retirement hishtalmut withdrawal-tax explorer · 5 detail-page walkthroughs · prior round of 6 retirement bug fixes · /argonaut 404 logged

1. What Argosy is

Argosy is two things glued together:

  1. An always-on Python orchestrator that watches your portfolio and the market continuously, with cadences running at minute / hour / daily / weekly / monthly / annual rhythms.
  2. A Next.js dashboard at localhost:1337 where you read state, approve proposals, and negotiate with the agent fleet.

The engine convenes a fleet of AI specialists (analysts, debate, risk officers, fund manager) to debate any non-trivial move. Each rationale is persisted, so months later you can ask why something happened and get a real answer.

The user-visible surface is split into 14 tabs, but the working flow only routes through 5-6 of them regularly. The rest are inspection / debugging tools.

3. How data gets in — the single mechanism

Tl;dr
/advisor chat is the universal portal for everything — bank statements, credit-card PDFs, screenshots, news, "I just got a bonus." Free-form text and the Attach button both flow through the same backend funnel (catalog_upload, SDD §17.1). One shortcut: /expenses ships a dedicated drag-drop tile for batch bank-statement ingest (same backend path, more convenient when you have 6 PDFs in a row). /files remains read-only — it's a catalog viewer, not an upload portal. Windfalls don't use any of these: they're auto-detected by diffing your monthly TSV (see §5).

Two ways to give Argosy new information from this surface:

  1. Free-form text in the chat. "I just got a 50K USD bonus paid today" or "We're expecting our second kid in November" or "I'm switching from Migdal to Clal." The Advisor extracts structured fields and updates the relevant gaps + propagates to the projections.
  2. Attach a file. Click the Attach pill (text/markdown, images, or PDFs — 10 MB per file, 20 MB total). Drop a Leumi statement PDF, a Discount Mastercard statement, a Schwab CSV, a screenshot of your payslip. The ingestion pipeline parses it server-side; the catalog row appears in /files; the relevant dashboard surface (e.g. /expenses) refreshes once parsing completes.

What's IN the gap tracker

The right-side panel groups ~75 required fields under 11 stages (Identity · Goals · Financial picture · Broker connections · Plan · Ops prefs · Estate · Insurance · Tax · Education · Special situations). Each row is colored:

  • fresh — value is recent enough for the field's freshness policy (e.g. portfolio_value monthly, identity annually)
  • stale — past freshness window; should be refreshed
  • missing — never filled

Currently: 76 fresh · 0 stale · 1 missing. The 1 missing is the "Last-reviewed year of legal will" gap surfaced on the Home page.

4. End-to-end flow: First-time setup

STEP 1
Open /advisor
The intake panel is the first thing you touch. Gap tracker on the right shows all 75 fields, mostly red (missing).
STEP 2
Click any missing row in the gap tracker
e.g. "Tax residency" → chat focuses on that field, asks you to fill it. Answer in plain English. Argosy extracts the structured value + marks the row fresh.
STEP 3
Repeat — or paste a bulk update
You can answer rows one-by-one OR paste a multi-field message ("DOB 1982-08-28, Israeli citizen, married, two kids ages 12 and 9, primary residence in Kiryat Tivon").
STEP 4
Attach your portfolio TSV / Schwab CSV
Drag onto chat or click Attach. The ingestion pipeline catalogs it under /files and the values land on /portfolio.
STEP 5
Bulk-upload 6-12 months of bank + credit statements
Two paths, same backend funnel: (a) drop PDFs in the <UploadStatementsCard> at the top of /expenses — faster for batches, multi-file drag-drop, per-file results render inline; (b) Attach one at a time on /advisor chat — better when you want to narrate context. Either way, the expense pipeline parses each statement and rows populate on /expenses.
STEP 6
Wait ~5-10 minutes for the first synthesis
The monthly_cycle loop kicks off a multi-agent synthesis once you have minimum-viable data. /plan populates with a draft.
STEP 7
Open /retirement
Now the long-term verdict has real data to chew on. Hero shows P(solvent at 95) under fat-tail-aware regime-switch MC.

5. End-to-end flow: Windfall (auto-detected — no buttons, no typing)

By design — Argosy notices, you don't push
Prior versions of this guide flagged "no '+ Add income event' button" as a UX hole. That framing is wrong. The current design treats the absence of an upload affordance as a feature: you maintain your existing TSV workflow (update_leumi_tsv.py + monthly drops into $ARGOSY_EXPENSE_SAMPLES_ROOT), and Argosy diffs consecutive snapshots to detect windfalls automatically. No buttons to find. No forms to fill.
STEP 1
Drop the latest Family Finances Status - YY MMM.tsv in your Portfolio/Resources folder
Same place you've always dropped them. Path resolves from $ARGOSY_EXPENSE_SAMPLES_ROOT (default D:/Google Drive/Family/Finances/Portfolio/Resources). You don't open the UI yet.
STEP 2
Backend detector fires automatically
argosy/services/retirement/windfall_detector.py diffs the two most-recent TSVs. Fires when the cash delta crosses $25K USD or ₪75K NIS. The classifier then matches equity sales within the same month to the cash delta (5% tolerance) and tags the source as rsu_sale, stock_sale, or unclear.
STEP 3
Open / — banner is already there
Home renders a windfall banner near the top with the cash delta, classification badge, and matching-sales summary (e.g. "NVDA -1040 + SGOV -350"). Tone is amber when classification is unclear (needs your input), neutral when Argosy resolved it.
STEP 4
Click See allocation plan →
Deep-links to /retirement#windfall. The full WindfallCard sits at the top of the page: hero verdict + the current vs plan target allocation table (one row per asset class in your TSV — typically 7-9) + three horizon columns (long / medium / short) with proposed instruments and dollar amounts.
STEP 5
Review the proposed split (60% long / 25% medium / 15% short)
Long-term proposals are deterministic: the allocator picks tickers you already hold to close the largest under-target plan gaps. The rendered rationale per proposal reads: "Closes the <asset class> plan-target gap ($<N>K under target). Preferred instrument: <ticker> (already in your portfolio)." Medium and short horizons render placeholder rows pending the agent-fleet synthesis hookup.
STEP 6
Accept / Defer per proposal
Buttons render on every proposal in every horizon column (long / medium / short). They're disabled right now — wiring through the action_engine (so an accepted proposal becomes a PrioritizedAction with a due date) is the next chunk of work. The card and the banner are read-only this round.
Still open — Advisor focus mode for "unclear" classifications
When the classifier tags a windfall as unclear (most-likely cause: a chunk of proceeds was redeployed inside the same month, so equity-sales total doesn't match cash delta within 5%), the banner currently just links to the retirement card. The plan calls for a deep-link into /advisor?focus=windfall_<id> that opens the chat with the event context pre-rendered and uses intake_extractor to classify your reply. That part hasn't shipped yet — see the resume note at docs/superpowers/plans/2026-05-28-windfall-flow-resume.md.
Open — No "verdict re-computed" confirmation
When a windfall is detected, Home banner appears and /retirement shows the card, but the RuinProbabilityHero at the top of /retirement doesn't show "Last re-computed at 14:32 (triggered by: windfall_detect)". Without that stamp the user can't tell whether the verdict reflects the new cash or the snapshot before it.
Suggested fix: stamp the hero with last-compute timestamp + trigger cause.

6. End-to-end flow: Monthly bank statement update

STEP 1
Get your monthly PDFs
Leumi NIS checking, Leumi USD, Discount Mastercard, Cal, Schwab, Max — any of the supported issuers.
STEP 2
Open /expenses and use the upload tile at the top
The <UploadStatementsCard> at the top of /expenses takes multi-file drag-drop. Drop one or more statements directly on the tile or click to pick. PDF / CSV / XLS / XLSX / TSV all accepted. (The /advisor chat Attach button still works too if you prefer it — same backend path through catalog_upload.)
STEP 3
Watch per-file results render inline
Each file gets a PARSED or FAILED pill plus stats: parser, transactions inserted, categorised, correlated, refunds matched. Failures show the error message. Max statements need the card last 4 — the tile surfaces an input automatically when a Max failure is detected.
STEP 4
Dashboard refreshes automatically
On any successful upload the overview re-fetches in the background — the Monthly sub-tab populates the new month and categories auto-assigned via the household_categorizer agent show up without a page reload.
UX hole #3 — no "last sync" indicator
/expenses shows the trailing-12 view but doesn't tell you when the latest statement was ingested. If you forgot to upload last month's Cal statement, the page silently shows incomplete data.
Suggested fix: per-source "Last statement: May 2026 (5 days ago)" indicator in the Sources sub-tab.
UX hole #4 — /files is still read-only
/files is a catalog viewer with no upload widget of its own. With the new <UploadStatementsCard> on /expenses the bank-statement flow is covered, but uploads of non-statement documents (a payslip, a tax letter, anything destined for the catalog rather than a parser) still have to go through the /advisor chat Attach button. /files still reads as "where I upload files" but is "where I see what I uploaded."
Suggested fix: add a general Upload button on /files that uses the same catalog_upload backend.

7. End-to-end flow: Credit card categorization

Argosy auto-categorizes each transaction via the household_categorizer agent (Opus-backed by default, per the accuracy-over-cost binding preference). Most rows are right out of the box; some need manual correction:

STEP 1
Open /expenses → Transactions sub-tab
Find the misclassified row (e.g. a hardware-store charge tagged "Other" should be "home_improvement").
STEP 2
Click the category cell — inline edit
Type the new category or pick from autocomplete. The choice is recorded in category_overrides and the agent learns from this for future similar merchants.
STEP 3 (alt)
Bulk-relabel via the Merchants sub-tab
If you want every "Cofix" charge to map to "coffee" instead of "restaurants", do it once at the merchant level and it back-propagates.

8. End-to-end flow: Plan → Retirement loop

You're right that the natural order is Plan first, then Retirement. They answer different questions:

PageAnswersTimescale
/plan"What should I DO this month/quarter?"Tactical · short + medium horizon
/retirement"Can I retire? When? At what risk?"Strategic · 30+ year horizon
STEP 1
Open /plan
Read the current draft. The FM agent's verdict (accept / reject) is at the top.
STEP 2
Walk the FM objections list
Each objection has AGREE / DISAGREE / DEFER + a counter-position textarea. The "Explain in plain English" toggle reveals a translated version if you don't follow the analyst jargon.
STEP 3
Hit "Start new round with my decisions"
Synthesis re-runs through all 5 phases (analysts → debate → synthesizer → risk → fund manager) with your AGREE/DISAGREE/DEFER decisions as user_directive. Takes ~70 min for the full Opus stack.
STEP 4
Once the new draft is accepted, open /retirement
The plan's accepted assumptions (mu, sigma, withdrawal policy, retirement age) are now your baseline. /retirement tells you whether those assumptions actually survive the 30-year fat-tail Monte Carlo.
STEP 5
If /retirement says OFF_TRACK, go back to /plan
Re-negotiate. Disagree with the FM objection that's now load-bearing (e.g. "delay retirement to 52"). The two pages bounce against each other until they agree.

9. / Home in detail

What each block does:

  • Anchor banner — version + agent count + cadence count + mode badges. Mode is paper / live / queue_only.
  • Fleet self-review — automatic post-synthesis audit of the agent fleet's own output (10 deterministic detectors). Link goes to /fleet-review/[id].
  • Daily-brief card — stitched from the day's gap deltas + investor-event signal + watchlist anomalies. "Review monthly plan" CTA → /plan.
  • Action items widget — pulled from the active plan's short + medium horizon. Bucketed OVERDUE / TODAY / DUE_SOON / UPCOMING.
  • Synthesis history (below the fold) — accordion of every prior agent-fleet run with cost + agent count + timestamp.

10. /advisor Advisor in detail

Layout:

  • Top banner — "Draft plan ready · 19 delta(s) vs. last month" + Review now CTA.
  • Plan in scope bar — which intake-upload doc is being used as the baseline + a Re-distill button.
  • Conversation panel — Cascade footer ("0 agents · $0.0000 · 0.0s") shows multi-agent cost of your last turn. Type a question, drop a file, paste a screenshot.
  • Attach pill — text/markdown, images, or PDFs. 10MB/file, 20MB/turn.
  • Gap tracker — 76 fresh / 0 stale / 1 missing. Rows grouped by stage. Click any row → chat focuses there.
Why this is the data portal
Every other dashboard READS from the data Argosy already has. Only the Advisor WRITES to it. Files = catalog (read-only), Expenses = parsed transactions (read-only), Portfolio = latest snapshot (read-only). If you want to change anything, you do it here.

13. /plan Plan in detail

The page lifecycle:

  1. Monthly cycle fires (1st of month) — 5-phase synthesis: 10 analysts → 2 debate teams → synthesizer → 3 risk officers → fund manager. ~30-70 min depending on stack.
  2. FM verdict lands — either ACCEPTED or REJECTED with N objections.
  3. Page renders the draft — rejection summary + per-objection details, severity-color-coded.
  4. Per-objection toggle — AGREE · DISAGREE · DEFER. DISAGREE opens a counter-position textarea. "Discuss with [analyst]" launches an FM↔analyst dialogue.
  5. "Start new round with my decisions" — re-runs synthesis with your toggle decisions as user_directive. Loop continues until FM accepts or you accept anyway.

18. Detail pages reached by click-through

These surfaces aren't in the top nav — you land on them by clicking a link from Home, /portfolio, /plan, /retirement, or one of the banners. They're documented here so the next time a banner says "Read report →" or a synthesis row says "Drill in →" you know what you're getting.

/decisions/<id> — Synthesis replay (primary debug surface)

The deepest view in the app. Lands here from the Home page's DecisionAccordion "Drill in →" links and from the in-flight-synthesis banner. One page per decision_runs.id.

Top: a VerdictCard showing the FM's final verdict (APPROVE / APPROVE_WITH_CONDITIONS / REJECT) + the decision_kind (plan_revision, trade_proposal, delta_pushback, daily_brief) + duration in human terms (e.g. "38 min" — and yes, the page corrects for the SQLite-naive-timestamp UTC-offset bug so the duration is accurate regardless of your local timezone).

Middle: the FM-rooted Agent Tree. Every agent that ran is a node; parent→child edges reflect orchestration (FM at root, sub-agents below). Each node renders agent role + status (ok / degraded / failed / skipped) + confidence + a one-line excerpt. Click to drill into the full prompt/response/citations for that one run. The codex_second_opinion node is special: it carries structured cross-engine findings (BLOCKER / AMBER / YELLOW) that are surfaced inline instead of buried in the transcript.

Bottom: Cost breakdown. Total $-spend for the run, split two ways — by phase (Phase 1 analysts / Phase 2 debates / Phase 3 synth / Phase 4 risk / Phase 4.5 codex / Phase 5 FM) and by top role. Useful when a synthesis runs hot and you want to know where the budget went. Below: a Mermaid sequence diagram of the whole flow if you want the alternative view.

When to come here
Three real reasons: (1) "why did the FM reject?" — the verdict card + tree tell you in one screen. (2) "what did codex flag in round 4.5?" — drill into the codex_second_opinion node. (3) "where did this synthesis spend its money?" — the cost breakdown at the bottom.

/fleet-review — Self-review trends (the list view)

The Home banner links to a specific report at /fleet-review/<id>. The list-index at /fleet-review (no id) shows the 30-day trend across all reports — useful for "is the AMBER count going up over time?" without having to open each report.

Top: a stacked-area chart over the last 30 days. RED layered on top of AMBER on top of YELLOW so a single RED spike doesn't get hidden inside a tall YELLOW band. X-axis is "MM-DD HH:mm" (post-synthesis reports cluster around plan revisions, so time-of-day matters).

Below the chart: persistent findings — the specific detectors that keep tripping across recent runs, named explicitly so the fix priority is obvious without opening each report.

Bottom: the most-recent 50 reports as clickable rows, each linking to its /fleet-review/<id> viewer.

When to come here
When the Home banner says "21 findings — 1 RED · 9 AMBER · 11 YELLOW" but you want trend context. Or when you suspect a detector is over-firing and want to see if it's the same finding repeated 12 times.

/anomalies/<id> — Full anomaly viewer

The Home banner surfaces the first RED anomaly from the most-recent report; /anomalies/<id> is where the rest live. Lands here from the banner's "view details →" link.

Each report row shows: severity pill (RED / AMBER / YELLOW), the watchlist entry name (e.g. "card-2923-fee-waiver"), the observation in plain English, suggested action, "last seen" timestamp, and the agent's confidence.

Below the anomaly list: a watchlist-status snapshot — per-entry state (NORMAL / ALERT / RESOLVED / UNKNOWN) so you can see what the agent was monitoring at the moment this report fired.

When to come here
The motivating use case: Card 2923's fee-waiver promotion expires. The anomaly_detection agent notices the discount line disappeared from your Discount Bank statement, fires a RED report, the Home banner links here. You see "card-2923-fee-waiver · ALERT · last seen 2026-04-30." That's the moment of truth.

/positions — Per-holding thesis cards

One card per holding: ticker · current shares · current weight % · USD value. Each card carries the agent fleet's verdict (HOLD / BUY / TRIM / SELL / ADD) + conviction (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) + a paragraph of reasoning + cited sources. Pulled from the pending plan draft (or the accepted plan when no draft is in flight).

Same component is also rendered inside /portfolio — /positions is the permalink so older bookmarks keep working. Easier to land on directly if all you want is "what does the fleet think about NVDA right now?"

When to come here
Tactical decisions about one holding. "Should I trim NVDA before earnings?" lands on this card, reads the thesis + cited sources, decides. (The actual sell still goes through /decide + /proposals.)

/onboarding + /intake — First-login + legacy redirect

/onboarding is the new-tenant landing page. Takes an email + a setup token (from the admin or the invite URL's ?token= param), signs the user in via NextAuth, and drops them on the home page where the Advisor's gap-tracker takes over (see §4). Off-nav by design — you'd only land here on first-login of a fresh tenant.

/intake is a thin redirect to /advisor. The old 6-stage interview was replaced by the persistent advisor relationship (gap-tracker sidebar + free-form Q&A) in the Phase 1 reframe; this stub stays so older bookmarks land on the right surface.

When to come here
Realistically: never, in single-user mode. Worth knowing about if you ever set up a second tenant (e.g. partner account) — the new tenant's first stop is /onboarding.

20. Holes still in the flow (not yet fixed)

These are real gaps that the per-tab walkthroughs surfaced. None block usage; all worsen UX. Numbering reflects the post-windfall state — the old Hole #1 ("Add income event isn't discoverable") was closed by the windfall auto-detect flow, see §19.

Hole #1 — No "verdict re-computed" confirmation
When a trigger fires (including the new windfall detector), the verdict re-runs server-side but no visible "Last re-computed at 14:32 (3 min ago, triggered by: windfall_detect)" stamp on the RuinProbabilityHero.
Suggested fix: stamp the hero with last-compute timestamp + trigger cause.
Hole #2 — Windfall Accept/Defer is read-only
The WindfallCard on /retirement renders Accept/Defer buttons on every proposal (long / medium / short horizons), but they're disabled with a tooltip. Wiring through the existing action_engine (so an accepted proposal becomes a PrioritizedAction with severity HIGH and a due date, surfaced in the home-page action-items widget) is the next chunk.
Suggested fix: add POST /api/retirement/windfall/accept + /defer endpoints + persistence; light up the buttons.
Hole #3 — Windfall classification dialogue not wired
When the detector tags a windfall as unclear, the banner currently just deep-links to /retirement#windfall. The resume note (docs/superpowers/plans/2026-05-28-windfall-flow-resume.md) sketches a focus-mode dialogue on /advisor that opens with the event context as the first message and extracts the user's clarification (bonus, gift, in-month redeployment) into structured state. Suggested fix: design + ship the focus-mode flow per the resume note.
Hole #4 — /expenses has no "last sync" per source
If you forgot to upload May 2026's Cal statement, /expenses silently shows incomplete data.
Suggested fix: per-source freshness badge in the Sources sub-tab.
Hole #5 — /files is read-only despite the name
Files = catalog, not upload portal. Users naturally try to upload here.
Suggested fix: add an Upload button on /files that calls the same catalog_upload backend the Advisor chat uses, OR rename the tab to "Catalog."
Hole #6 — Parsing failures are silent
If a PDF is in an unsupported format (older SpreadsheetML Leumi files), the parser logs "?? UnknownFormatError" but the Advisor chat shows a generic message. No visible error in /files.
Suggested fix: per-file parse-status column on /files with a "Re-try" button.

Already logged. Pre-existing endpoint gap, separate from retirement work.
