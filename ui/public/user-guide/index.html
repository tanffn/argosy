Argosy — User Guide (end-to-end)

How you actually use the whole system — orientation, end-to-end flows, per-tab detail.

Audience: Ariel (single-user mode) Screenshots: Playwright + Chromium @ 1400×900 Covers: 14 top-nav tabs + 5 click-through detail pages

1. What Argosy is

Argosy is two things glued together:

  1. An always-on Python orchestrator that watches your portfolio and the market continuously, with cadences running at minute / hour / daily / weekly / monthly / annual rhythms.
  2. A Next.js dashboard at localhost:1337 where you read state, approve proposals, and negotiate with the agent fleet.

The engine convenes a fleet of AI specialists (analysts, debate, risk officers, fund manager) to debate any non-trivial move. Each rationale is persisted, so months later you can ask why something happened and get a real answer.

The user-visible surface is split into 14 tabs, but the working flow only routes through 5-6 of them regularly. The rest are inspection / debugging tools.

3. How data gets in — the single mechanism

Tl;dr
Three upload tiles + one universal chat, all flowing through the same backend funnel (catalog_upload, SDD §17.1):
  • /expenses → monthly bank statements (Leumi, Schwab, Discount, Cal, Max — PDF/CSV/XLS).
  • /portfolio → monthly portfolio-status snapshot (the Family Finances Status TSV or a raw Leumi monthly XLS — the XLS auto-pairs with your Osh statement via DB lookup).
  • /files → anything else that should land in the catalog (payslip, tax letter, screenshots).
  • /advisor chat → free-form text + Attach for the long-tail (a quote, a news story, "I'm switching from Migdal to Clal"). Use this when none of the three structured tiles fit.

Two ways to give Argosy new information from this surface:

  1. Free-form text in the chat. "I just got a 50K USD bonus paid today" or "We're expecting our second kid in November" or "I'm switching from Migdal to Clal." The Advisor extracts structured fields and updates the relevant gaps + propagates to the projections.
  2. Attach a file. Click the Attach pill (text/markdown, images, or PDFs — 10 MB per file, 20 MB total). Drop a Leumi statement PDF, a Discount Mastercard statement, a Schwab CSV, a screenshot of your payslip. The ingestion pipeline parses it server-side; the catalog row appears in /files; the relevant dashboard surface (e.g. /expenses) refreshes once parsing completes.

What's IN the gap tracker

The right-side panel groups ~75 required fields under 11 stages (Identity · Goals · Financial picture · Broker connections · Plan · Ops prefs · Estate · Insurance · Tax · Education · Special situations). Each row is colored:

  • fresh — value is recent enough for the field's freshness policy (e.g. portfolio_value monthly, identity annually)
  • stale — past freshness window; should be refreshed
  • missing — never filled

Currently: 76 fresh · 0 stale · 1 missing. The 1 missing is the "Last-reviewed year of legal will" gap surfaced on the Home page.

4. End-to-end flow: First-time setup

STEP 1
Open /advisor
The intake panel is the first thing you touch. Gap tracker on the right shows all 75 fields, mostly red (missing).
STEP 2
Click any missing row in the gap tracker
e.g. "Tax residency" → chat focuses on that field, asks you to fill it. Answer in plain English. Argosy extracts the structured value + marks the row fresh.
STEP 3
Repeat — or paste a bulk update
You can answer rows one-by-one OR paste a multi-field message ("DOB 1982-08-28, Israeli citizen, married, two kids ages 12 and 9, primary residence in Kiryat Tivon").
STEP 4
Attach your portfolio TSV / Schwab CSV
Drag onto chat or click Attach. The ingestion pipeline catalogs it under /files and the values land on /portfolio.
STEP 5
Bulk-upload 6-12 months of bank + credit statements
Two paths, same backend funnel: (a) drop PDFs in the <UploadStatementsCard> at the top of /expenses — faster for batches, multi-file drag-drop, per-file results render inline; (b) Attach one at a time on /advisor chat — better when you want to narrate context. Either way, the expense pipeline parses each statement and rows populate on /expenses.
STEP 6
Open /plan — review the first draft from the agent fleet
A draft lands within ~5-10 minutes once minimum-viable data is in place (the monthly_cycle loop kicks off automatically; you'll see an "in-flight synthesis" banner on Home while it runs). On /plan you'll see: (a) the Fund Manager's verdict (APPROVE / APPROVE_WITH_CONDITIONS / REJECT), (b) per-objection AGREE / DISAGREE / DEFER toggles + counter-position textareas — these capture your stance on each FM concern, (c) a "Start new round with my decisions" CTA at the bottom that re-runs the fleet with your stance threaded into every agent's prompt. Click through a few objections to set your position, then either accept the draft as-is or kick a new round.
STEP 7
Open /retirement
Now the long-term verdict has real data to chew on. Hero shows P(solvent at 95) under fat-tail-aware regime-switch MC.

5. End-to-end flow: Monthly cadence — drop both halves

The recurring monthly workflow is two drops, in any order. That's it. Everything downstream — categorisation, snapshot persistence, windfall detection, replan triggers — fires automatically.

Half A — Bank statements → /expenses

STEP A1
Get your monthly statement PDFs
Leumi NIS checking, Leumi USD, Discount Mastercard, Cal, Schwab, Max — any of the supported issuers. PDF, CSV, XLS, XLSX, or TSV all work.
STEP A2
Drag-drop onto the tile at the top of /expenses
Multi-file in one shot is fine. The route detects the issuer per file and dispatches to the right parser. Max statements need the card last-4 — the tile surfaces an input automatically when a Max failure is detected.
STEP A3
Per-file results render inline
Each file gets a PARSED or FAILED pill plus stats: parser, transactions inserted, categorised, correlated, refunds matched. Failures show the error message. The dashboard re-fetches automatically; the Monthly sub-tab populates the new month + categories assigned via household_categorizer show up without a page reload.

Half B — Portfolio snapshot → /portfolio

STEP B1
Click Generate TSV now on /portfolio
Argosy composes a fresh Family Finances Status TSV from current state: position structure from the most recent prior TSV at the scan root; Leumi NIS + USD cash rows refreshed from the latest leumi_osh and leumi_usd bank-statement closing balances in expense_statements; allocation-block currents recomputed; snapshot_date bumped to today. Writes under $ARGOSY_EXPENSE_SAMPLES_ROOT with the canonical name. Returns the persisted path, the refreshed cash figures, and any warnings (e.g. if a bank statement is missing the cash row carries forward verbatim).
STEP B2 (input)
When you have a fresh Leumi monthly XLS, drop it onto the upload tile
The route format-sniffs the upload. The XLS carries positions only; Argosy auto-pairs it with the matching Leumi Osh statement from your /expenses uploads via DB lookup (±15d window). Bidirectional: if the XLS lands before the Osh, the upload queues as AWAITING OSH; the pair completes when the matching Osh statement subsequently ingests. The synthesized TSV becomes the new "prior" that subsequent Generate-TSV clicks pull from.
STEP B3
Per-action outcome renders inline
Generate-TSV: GENERATED pill + the persisted path + refreshed Leumi NIS / USD cash figures + any warnings. Upload: PERSISTED + detector status (ok / skipped / failed / pending_pair) + event (cash-delta over threshold ⇒ deep-link to /retirement#windfall) + AWAITING OSH pill when an XLS is queued.

What fires automatically off Half B — the windfall side effect

When Half B reveals a cash delta over $25K USD or ₪75K NIS between consecutive snapshots, the windfall detector tags an event and the allocator produces a ranked plan. You did not push anything; the same B2 drop produced both the snapshot persistence and the windfall surface.

SIDE-EFFECT 1
Home banner appears at /
Headline number + classification badge (RSU SALE / STOCK SALE / UNCLEAR) + matching-sales summary (e.g. "NVDA -1040 + SGOV -350"). Tone is amber when classification is unclear (the classifier couldn't match equity sales to the cash delta within 5% — likely some proceeds got redeployed in-month).
SIDE-EFFECT 2
Click "See allocation plan →" to land on /proposals#allocation
The full allocation queue: hero verdict driven by the backend allocator's canonical rationale + the current vs plan-target allocation table (DESTINATION pills on under-target rows = where the windfall should go) + three horizon columns (long deterministic / medium + short agent-fleet placeholders) + matching-sales drilldown.
SIDE-EFFECT 3
Accept or Defer per proposal
Live buttons. Accept persists a row in windfall_actions with decided_status='accepted'; Defer same with optional due_date. Already-decided proposals render as "✓ Accepted" / "↻ Deferred · due 2026-06-15" pills inline on re-fetch. The next step (promotion of an accepted row into an action_engine PrioritizedAction on the home-page action-items widget) is queued — see §18 Hole #2.

Argosy doesn't have a "+ Add income event" button — classification is derived from snapshot diffs, not entered by hand. The upload tiles on /expenses and /portfolio are the only data-entry points; everything else (windfall classification, allocation suggestions) is computed downstream. If the classifier can't resolve the source automatically (the unclear case), answer in free-form chat on /advisor.

Unallocated cash — the continuous side of the windfall flow

The windfall detector handles the cliff case (one-off cash spikes over $25K USD / ₪75K NIS — RSU sales, bonuses, gifts). The routine case — your monthly paycheck residue piles up in the bank — is below that threshold but still wants allocation. Argosy surfaces this as a separate tile on /portfolio: the <UnallocatedCashCard>.

TRIGGER
Self-tuning, no dollar threshold
The tile fires when current cash exceeds your plan-target cash by a configurable ratio (default 1.5x). Pulled from the latest snapshot's Current allocation: block — the cash row's current_k_usd vs target_k. If you're within 1.5x of target, the tile renders nothing.
DISPLAY
Excess + suggested buys
When fired, the card shows the headline (e.g. "Cash sits 2.3x your plan target ($120K vs $52K). Proposed allocation closes the largest plan-target gaps.") plus three stat cells (current / target / ratio) and a ranked list of suggested buys — same allocator logic as the windfall flow, reusing _allocate_long_term() from the windfall allocator with the excess as input.
DECISION
Accept/Defer wiring pending
The buy suggestions render but don't yet have Accept/Defer buttons (queued — they will reuse the existing windfall_actions table since the proposal shape is identical). For now, treat the tile as a "here's what Argosy thinks" advisory; execute manually in your broker.

6. End-to-end flow: Credit card categorization

Argosy auto-categorizes each transaction via the household_categorizer agent (Opus-backed by default, per the accuracy-over-cost binding preference). Most rows are right out of the box; some need manual correction:

STEP 1
Open /expenses → Transactions sub-tab
Find the misclassified row (e.g. a hardware-store charge tagged "Other" should be "home_improvement").
STEP 2
Click the category cell — inline edit
Type the new category or pick from autocomplete. The choice is recorded in category_overrides and the agent learns from this for future similar merchants.
STEP 3 (alt)
Bulk-relabel via the Merchants sub-tab
If you want every "Cofix" charge to map to "coffee" instead of "restaurants", do it once at the merchant level and it back-propagates.

7. End-to-end flow: Plan → Retirement loop

The natural order is Plan first, then Retirement. They answer different questions:

PageAnswersTimescale
/plan"What should I DO this month/quarter?"Tactical · short + medium horizon
/retirement"Can I retire? When? At what risk?"Strategic · 30+ year horizon
STEP 1
Open /plan
Read the current draft. The FM agent's verdict (accept / reject) is at the top.
STEP 2
Walk the FM objections list
Each objection has AGREE / DISAGREE / DEFER + a counter-position textarea. The "Explain in plain English" toggle reveals a translated version if you don't follow the analyst jargon.
STEP 3
Hit "Start new round with my decisions"
Synthesis re-runs through all 5 phases (analysts → debate → synthesizer → risk → fund manager) with your AGREE/DISAGREE/DEFER decisions as user_directive. Takes ~70 min for the full Opus stack.
STEP 4
Once the new draft is accepted, open /retirement
The plan's accepted assumptions (mu, sigma, withdrawal policy, retirement age) are now your baseline. /retirement tells you whether those assumptions actually survive the 30-year fat-tail Monte Carlo.
STEP 5
If /retirement says OFF_TRACK, go back to /plan
Re-negotiate. Disagree with the FM objection that's now load-bearing (e.g. "delay retirement to 52"). Run another synthesis round until /retirement clears.

8. / Home in detail

What each block does:

  • Anchor banner — version + agent count + cadence count + mode badges. Mode is paper / live / queue_only.
  • Fleet self-review — automatic post-synthesis audit of the agent fleet's own output (10 deterministic detectors). Link goes to /fleet-review/[id].
  • Daily-brief card — stitched from the day's gap deltas + investor-event signal + watchlist anomalies. "Review monthly plan" CTA → /plan.
  • Action items widget — pulled from the active plan's short + medium horizon. Bucketed OVERDUE / TODAY / DUE_SOON / UPCOMING.
  • Synthesis history (below the fold) — accordion of every prior agent-fleet run with cost + agent count + timestamp.

The monthly snapshot upload tile (2026-05-29)

Top of /portfolio ships a drag-drop tile that accepts the monthly Family Finances Status - YY MMM.tsv file. Drop it in (or click to pick); the route persists the TSV under ARGOSY_EXPENSE_SAMPLES_ROOT and synchronously runs the windfall detector against the prior month's snapshot. The upload tile accepts both TSV files and Leumi monthly portfolio XLS exports. XLS uploads pair with the matching Leumi Osh current-account statement for the cash row (±15-day match window, bidirectional); until the pair lands the XLS upload renders as AWAITING OSH. Tones are picked so HOLD (the most-common verdict) reads as background; BUY/ADD shine emerald, TRIM amber, SELL rose. 12. /plan Plan in detail

The page lifecycle:

  1. Monthly cycle fires (1st of month) — 5-phase synthesis: 10 analysts → 2 debate teams → synthesizer → 3 risk officers → fund manager. ~30-70 min depending on stack.
  2. FM verdict lands — either ACCEPTED or REJECTED with N objections.
  3. Page renders the draft — rejection summary + per-objection details, severity-color-coded.
  4. Per-objection toggle — AGREE · DISAGREE · DEFER. DISAGREE opens a counter-position textarea. "Discuss with [analyst]" launches an FM↔analyst dialogue.

17. Detail pages reached by click-through

These surfaces aren't in the top nav — you land on them by clicking a link from Home, /portfolio, /plan, /retirement, or one of the banners. They're documented here so the next time a banner says "Read report →" or a synthesis row says "Drill in →" you know what you're getting.

/decisions/<id> — Synthesis replay (primary debug surface)

The deepest view in the app. Lands here from the Home page's DecisionAccordion "Drill in →" links and from the in-flight-synthesis banner. One page per decision_runs.id.

Top: a VerdictCard showing the FM's final verdict (APPROVE / APPROVE_WITH_CONDITIONS / REJECT) + the decision_kind (plan_revision, trade_proposal, delta_pushback, daily_brief) + duration in human terms (e.g. "38 min" — and yes, the page corrects for the SQLite-naive-timestamp UTC-offset bug so the duration is accurate regardless of your local timezone).

Middle: the FM-rooted Agent Tree. Every agent that ran is a node; parent→child edges reflect orchestration (FM at root, sub-agents below). Each node renders agent role + status (ok / degraded / failed / skipped) + confidence + a one-line excerpt. Click to drill into the full prompt/response/citations for that one run. The codex_second_opinion node is special: it carries structured cross-engine findings (BLOCKER / AMBER / YELLOW) that are surfaced inline instead of buried in the transcript.

Bottom: Cost breakdown. Total $-spend for the run, split two ways — by phase (Phase 1 analysts / Phase 2 debates / Phase 3 synth / Phase 4 risk / Phase 4.5 codex / Phase 5 FM) and by top role. Useful when a synthesis runs hot and you want to know where the budget went. Below: a Mermaid sequence diagram of the whole flow if you want the alternative view.

When to come here
Three real reasons: (1) "why did the FM reject?" — the verdict card + tree tell you in one screen. (2) "what did codex flag in round 4.5?" — drill into the codex_second_opinion node. (3) "where did this synthesis spend its money?" — the cost breakdown at the bottom.

/fleet-review — Self-review trends (the list view)

The Home banner links to a specific report at /fleet-review/<id>. The list-index at /fleet-review (no id) shows the 30-day trend across all reports — useful for "is the AMBER count going up over time?" without having to open each report.

Top: a stacked-area chart over the last 30 days. RED layered on top of AMBER on top of YELLOW so a single RED spike doesn't get hidden inside a tall YELLOW band. X-axis is "MM-DD HH:mm" (post-synthesis reports cluster around plan revisions, so time-of-day matters).

Below the chart: persistent findings — the specific detectors that keep tripping across recent runs, named explicitly so the fix priority is obvious without opening each report.

Bottom: the most-recent 50 reports as clickable rows, each linking to its /fleet-review/<id> viewer.

When to come here
When the Home banner says "21 findings — 1 RED · 9 AMBER · 11 YELLOW" but you want trend context. Or when you suspect a detector is over-firing and want to see if it's the same finding repeated 12 times.

/anomalies/<id> — Full anomaly viewer

The Home banner surfaces the first RED anomaly from the most-recent report; /anomalies/<id> is where the rest live. Lands here from the banner's "view details →" link.

Each report row shows: severity pill (RED / AMBER / YELLOW), the watchlist entry name (e.g. "card-2923-fee-waiver"), the observation in plain English, suggested action, "last seen" timestamp, and the agent's confidence.

Below the anomaly list: a watchlist-status snapshot — per-entry state (NORMAL / ALERT / RESOLVED / UNKNOWN) so you can see what the agent was monitoring at the moment this report fired.

When to come here
The motivating use case: Card 2923's fee-waiver promotion expires. The anomaly_detection agent notices the discount line disappeared from your Discount Bank statement, fires a RED report, the Home banner links here. You see "card-2923-fee-waiver · ALERT · last seen 2026-04-30." That's the moment of truth.

/positions — Per-holding thesis cards

One card per holding: ticker · current shares · current weight % · USD value. Each card carries the agent fleet's verdict (HOLD / BUY / TRIM / SELL / ADD) + conviction (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) + a paragraph of reasoning + cited sources. Pulled from the pending plan draft (or the accepted plan when no draft is in flight).

Same component is also rendered inside /portfolio — /positions is the permalink so older bookmarks keep working. Easier to land on directly if all you want is "what does the fleet think about NVDA right now?"

When to come here
Tactical decisions about one holding. "Should I trim NVDA before earnings?" lands on this card, reads the thesis + cited sources, decides. (The actual sell still goes through /consult + /proposals.)

/onboarding + /intake — First-login + legacy redirect

/onboarding is the new-tenant landing page. Takes an email + a setup token (from the admin or the invite URL's ?token= param), signs the user in via NextAuth, and drops them on the home page where the Advisor's gap-tracker takes over (see §4). Not in the main nav — first-login of a fresh tenant only.

/intake is a thin redirect to /advisor. Intake happens inside the persistent Advisor relationship (gap-tracker sidebar + free-form Q&A); this stub stays so older bookmarks land on the right surface.

When to come here
Realistically: never, in single-user mode. Worth knowing about if you ever set up a second tenant (e.g. partner account) — the new tenant's first stop is /onboarding.

18. Known gaps

Things that don't work yet, or that the UI doesn't help with. None block usage; all worsen UX.

Hole #1 — Windfall classification dialogue not wired (Advisor focus-mode)
When the detector tags a windfall as unclear, the banner currently just deep-links to /retirement#windfall. The design sketched in docs/superpowers/plans/2026-05-28-windfall-flow-resume.md calls for a focus-mode dialogue on /advisor that opens with the event context as the first message and extracts the user's clarification (bonus, gift, in-month redeployment) via intake_extractor into a new income_events table. Concrete schema + extractor agent + advisor route shape are TBD.
Suggested fix: design + ship the focus-mode flow per the resume note.
Hole #2 — Windfall Accept/Defer doesn't yet promote into an action
The Accept and Defer buttons on the WindfallCard record a row in the windfall_actions table; "✓ Accepted" or "↻ Deferred · due 2026-06-15" pills appear after you click. Accepted proposals do not yet become PrioritizedAction rows on the home-page action-items widget.
Suggested fix: background job that walks windfall_actions rows with decided_status='accepted' + proposal_id IS NULL, calls action_engine.create_from_windfall, fills in the FK.
Hole #4 — No site-wide search
If you're hunting for a specific past decision ("the day we trimmed NVDA in March"), you have to know to go to /audit. No site-wide search box.
Suggested fix: ⌘K / Ctrl-K palette searching audit_log + transactions + plans.
